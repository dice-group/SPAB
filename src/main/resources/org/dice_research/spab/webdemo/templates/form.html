The SPAB algorithm generates regular expressions, which represent generalized SPARQL statements. It iteratively generates candidates of regular expressions. In each iteration, the currently best known candidate is selected and its children (refined regular expressions) are generated.

For the selection of the best candidate, a score for every generated candidate is calculated. Therefore, it is tested, if the regular expression of a respective candidate matches pre-defined SPARQL queries. A regular expression is tested to match as many queries as possible from the positive set of SPARQL queries and as few queries as possible from the negative set. This is implemented by F-measure (F1 score) classification using precision and recall.
